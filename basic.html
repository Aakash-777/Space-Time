Space Time

The Sun contains 99.86% of the total mass in our solar system.
The Milky Way galaxy is so large that even at the speed of light, it would take 100,000 years to travel across it.
Jupiter is so massive that it could fit more than 1,300 Earths inside it.
A day on Venus is longer than a year on Earth. Venus has an extremely slow rotation on its axis.
Astronauts on the International Space Station (ISS) experience 16 sunrises and sunsets every day.
Neutron stars are so dense that a teaspoon of neutron star material would weigh about 6 billion tons on Earth.
The Great Red Spot on Jupiter is a massive storm that has been raging for at least 350 years.
Astronauts' height can change in space. Without the effects of gravity, the spine elongates, causing astronauts to grow a few inches taller.
The moon is gradually moving away from Earth at a rate of about 1.5 inches (3.8 cm) per year.
